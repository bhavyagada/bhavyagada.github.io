Expenses

Your expenses will vary based on your preferences and lifestyle. However, the following points can provide you with an estimate.

Currently, the total fees for attending UCR is ~$11,000/quarter (international fees, tuition fees, insurance, miscellaneous), which can be brought down to ~$9700/quarter (Email me for details).

Rent is affordable in Riverside, and sharing a room with someone can save you more. I personally share a room and only spend $570/month (rent, utilities and internet). On-campus housing sells out fast, so apply quickly. Check out this resource for off-campus housing.

Cooking at home and using UCR resources like R'Pantry and Gift Cards can help save money on grocery expenses. On average, I only spend around $40-$50/month on food and groceries.

Academics

You typically have two career options to consider: Research (PhD) or Industry.

If your goal is to pursue research, choosing UCR would be a wise decision as it is one of the top universities in terms of research activity according to CSRankings. Browse through the faculty listings which include distinguished professors recognized for their exceptional research work.

At UCR, academics lean towards preparing you for research. Most courses align with the professor's research rather than the subject matter itself, and may not be particularly useful if you wish to join the industry after graduating. However, this can be said for many universities.

On the bright side, it is easy to earn good grades in many courses just by attending classes and doing your assignments & projects well (WITHOUT CHEATING!!). Courses are offered on a first come, first served basis, so plan ahead and decide on your desired path or specialization. It's also a good idea to have a backup plan.

In the fast paced quarter system, it is crucial to manage your time well. Procrastination will cause you a lot of stress. However, you can still make time for other activities, sports or travelling. Plus, you can complete your degree faster, saving you both time & money.

Job prospects

Finding an on-campus job can be challenging, but not impossible. New job postings are listed here regularly.

Start networking with professors before coming to the USA. They can offer research assistantship (GSR) and grader/reader jobs. They may also refer you to teaching assistantship (TA) jobs. Although you can only work with them starting from the 2nd quarter, building a relationship with professors early on is beneficial.

Grader/reader roles are easier to get if the professor knows you well. However, GSR positions require skills that align with the professor's research, so it's advisable to read about their research and reach out to those whose work interests you. TA positions are the most difficult to obtain, and luck plays a significant role in the process.

Internships are difficult to find in the current job market, especially for tech roles, as you may already be aware. However, if the market improves next year, you need not worry. Students from the previous batch at UCR (2021-2023) were able to secure internships at top companies like Apple, Meta and Playstation, among others. Opportunities will be available to you everywhere if you possess sufficient skills. Begin applying for internships before coming to the USA, keep your resume up-to-date, and network with alumni and recruiters on LinkedIn.

Similarly, the job market for full-time jobs will be very competitive, but students from the previous batch have been able to secure employment even in this difficult market. Your success in finding a job will depend on your skills and qualifications.

Riverside

Except for the scorching summers, the weather here is generally delightful.

Riverside has some cool places to explore like hiking trails, museums and other attractions.

There's not an abundance of activities within Riverside, but popular places like Los Angeles, San Diego and Las Vegas can be reached within a few hours' drive.

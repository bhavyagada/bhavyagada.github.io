Expenses

Your expenses will vary based on your preferences and lifestyle. However, the following points can provide you with an estimate.

Currently, the total fees for attending UCR is ~$11,000/quarter (international fees, tuition fees, insurace, miscellaneous). You can bring this cost down to ~$9700/quarter (Email me for details).

Rent is relatively affordable in Riverside, and you can save even more by sharing a room with someone. I personally share a room and only spend $570/month (rent, utilities and internet). You have the option of either on-campus or off-campus housing. If you prefer on-campus housing, be sure to apply fast, as they sell out quickly and can be difficult to secure later on. For off-campus housing, this is the best resource.

One way to save on food is by cooking at home, and UCR offers various options like R'Pantry and Gift Cards to help with grocery expenses. On average, I only spend around $40-$50/month on food and groceries.

Academics

There are generally two career paths for you to consider: Do research (PhD) or Join the Industry.

If your goal is to pursue research and eventually earn a PhD, choosing to attend this university would be a wise decision as it is one of the top universities in terms of research activity according to CSRankings. Browse through the faculty listings which include distinguished professors recognized for their exceptional research work, many of whom have received prestigious accolades like the test of time award.

At UCR, academics lean towards preparing you for research. Most courses align with the professor's research rather than the subject matter itself, and may not be particularly useful if you wish to join the industry after graduating. However, this can be said for many universities.

On the bright side, it is easy to earn an A or A+ grade in many courses if you regularly attend classes and do your assignments & projects well (WITHOUT CHEATING!!). Courses are offered on a first come, first served basis, so plan ahead and decide on your desired path or specialization. It's also a good idea to have a backup plan.

In the fast paced quarter system, it is crucial to manage your time well. Procrastination will cause you a lot of stress. However, you can still make time for other activities, sports or travelling. Plus, you can complete your degree faster, saving you both time & money.

Job prospects

In my experience, finding an on-campus job has been challenging, not just for me but for many of the students I know. However, it's not impossible. New on-campus job postings are always listed here. Keep applying, and eventually, you will land a job.

It's crucial to start networking with professors before coming to the USA. They offer jobs like research assistantship (GSR) and grader/reader roles. Although you can only work with them starting from the 2nd quarter, building a relationship with professors early on is beneficial. They may also refer you to teaching assistantship (TA) jobs. TA and GSR jobs come with fee waivers, which can reduce your student loans.

Regarding internships, the job market is currently challenging, especially for tech roles, as you may already be aware. Most students haven't been able to land an internship this year. However, if the market improves next year, you need not worry. Students from the previous batch at UCR (2021-2023) were able to secure internships at top companies like Apple, Meta and Playstation, among others. Opportunities will be available to you everywhere if you possess sufficient skills. I recommend that you begin applying for internships before coming to the USA, keep your resume up-to-date, and and network with alumni and recruiters on LinkedIn.

Similarly, the job market for full-time jobs will be very competitive, but students from the previous batch have been able to secure employment even in this difficult market. Your success in finding a job will depend on your skills and qualifications.

Riverside

Except for the scorching summers, the weather here is generally delightful.

Riverside has some cool places to explore like hiking trails, museums and other attractions.

There's not an abundance of activities within Riverside, but popular places like Los Angeles, San Diego and Las Vegas can be reached within a few hours' drive.
